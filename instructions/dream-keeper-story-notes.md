# Dream Keeper story — sources & open items (2026-07-06)

Page: `website/stories/dream-keeper/` (hand-authored parallax page, NOT generated by build.py).

## Verified facts used on the page

- M/V Dream Keeper (also reported as "DreamKeeper"), liveaboard dive yacht. Departed San Remigio
  ("San Remegio" in some reports), northern Cebu ~4 p.m. 27 Apr 2023; arrived off Tubbataha
  ~10 p.m. 29 Apr; capsized in a sudden squall the morning of Sun 30 Apr 2023.
  PCG command center received the report 6:49 a.m. via Coast Guard District Palawan.
- Aboard: 32 (12 guests, 15 crew, 5 divemasters). 28 rescued incl. 4 Chinese tourists;
  4 missing, all Filipinos: a divemaster, 2 passengers, and the yacht's owner.
- 1 May 2023: PCG confirmed squall as cause; oil slick spotted near the site, ~4 nautical miles
  from the Tubbataha sanctuary boundary; response team on standby. AFP Western Command: SAR
  intensified with Britten-Norman Islander, US P-8 Poseidon, 2× V-22 Osprey; BRP Felix Apolinario
  dispatched to join BRP Carlos Albert; naval helicopter from BRP Melchora Aquino considered.
- Tubbataha: mid-Sulu Sea ~150 km SE of Puerto Princesa; N+S atolls + Jessie Beazley Reef;
  ~97,000 ha; UNESCO WHS 1993; ~600 fish / 360 coral / 11 shark / 13 cetacean / 100 bird species;
  100 m walls; no-take park, ranger station only; season mid-Mar–mid-Jun; name from Sama
  "long reef exposed at low tide".

Sources: GMA News, Philstar, Manila Times, Manila Bulletin, Divernet, UNESCO whc.unesco.org/en/list/653,
tubbatahareefs.org (all linked in the page footer).
